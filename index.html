Seasonal tendencies
ICT teaches that certain months and quarters have historical delivery tendencies. Use this to align your macro bias with seasonal patterns.
Q1 (Jan–Mar)
Typically bullish. January marks the yearly opening price. ICT teaches the yearly open is a key reference — price above it in Q1 = strong bullish seasonal tendency. Watch for expansion moves off the yearly open.
Q2 (Apr–Jun)
Typically a retracement or distribution quarter. Often the yearly high or significant high forms in Q2. ICT notes this is when the algorithm delivers a retracement to build orders for the next leg.
Q3 (Jul–Sep)
Mixed — can trend or consolidate. August and September historically have lower volatility. ICT uses this period for accumulation watching. Lower timeframe setups are cleaner in July.
Q4 (Oct–Dec)
Strong trending quarter. October often marks a significant low or high. November-December historically bullish for indices. ICT notes the algorithm delivers aggressively in Q4 to close the yearly range.
Key monthly patterns
First 3 trading days of month: algorithm sets the monthly opening range. Mid-month (10th–15th): manipulation of monthly range. Last week: distribution or reversal as monthly positions are squared.
