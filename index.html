Seasonal tendencies
ICT teaches that certain months and quarters have historical delivery tendencies. Align your macro bias with seasonal patterns — fighting them is the most common macro mistake.
Q1 (Jan–Mar) — Bullish expansion
January yearly open is the primary reference. Price above it all of Q1 = strong bullish seasonal tendency. Watch for expansion moves off the yearly open range.
Q2 (Apr–Jun) — Retracement/distribution
Yearly high often forms in Q2. Algorithm retraces into discount to build orders. Lower your frequency. Focus on LTF precision.
Q3 (Jul–Sep) — Accumulation
August and September have lower institutional participation. July is the cleanest month of Q3. Reduce size and frequency in August–September.
Q4 (Oct–Dec) — Strong trending
October often marks a major low. November–December historically bullish for indices. Hold winners longer in Q4. High R setups more common.
Monthly delivery pattern
First 3 days = opening range. Days 10–15 = manipulation. Week 3 = distribution. Last week = avoid new entries.
